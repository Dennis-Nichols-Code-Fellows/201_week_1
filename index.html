Professional History and Goals

Currently, I am attending an immersive bootcamp in software development with Code Fellows. My plan is to gain competency in software development and data science skills to combine with my experience and passion for public health and environmental issues.

I have worked as public health professional and contractor for the CDC. I've learned a lot about how governmental scientific organizations function and am very thankful for the experience I've gained in this role.

My particular interests (and the subject of my work at CDC) are in issues of Water and Sanitation, future climate challenges, and the application of geographical methods and technologies to improve public health research and practice. One of my personal goals is to become bilingual - to that end I have dedicated myself to independent Spanish learning for the last several years. In past summers I've made progress down this path while working on an environmental sampling project in Bolivia and taking classes in Peru - from this summer through next I will gain more experience and training in Spanish by living in Colombia, Chile, and Mexico.

As a former nuclear reactor operator in the US Navy, I bring systems thinking, experience in multidisciplinary teams, and an ability to work in environments with a demanding operational tempo to any role. I am looking for a position with a social/health/environment-focused organization where my problem-solving skills are challenged and I have the opportunity to wear many hats.
